Month 1: UX Design Fundamentals (24 hours)

  • Day 1: UX Fundamentals & Business Requirement Gathering (2 hours)
    • Overview of UX principles and how design impacts business.
    • Techniques to gather business needs and align them with design goals.
  • Day 2: User Research (2 hours)
    • Learn methods for user research: surveys, interviews, observations.
  • Day 3: User Interview Techniques (2 hours)
    • Best practices for conducting user interviews and extracting valuable insights.
  • Day 1: Research Data Analysis (2 hours)
    • Organizing and interpreting user research data to derive actionable insights.
  • Day 2: Competitor Benchmarking (2 hours)
    • Compare your design with competitors to identify strengths and weaknesses.
  • Day 3: Heuristic Analysis (2 hours)
    • Conduct usability evaluation based on heuristic principles.
  • Day 1: Ideation Techniques (2 hours)
    • Learn up to 7 ideation techniques, such as brainstorming, SCAMPER, etc.
  • Day 2: Whiteboarding & Journey Maps (2 hours)
    • Visualize ideas and create user journey maps to map out the user experience.
  • Day 3: Motivation & Empathy Mapping (2 hours)
    • Explore motivation mapping and empathy mapping processes.
  • Day 1: Information Architecture & Navigation Design (2 hours)
    • Learn to structure information and create intuitive navigation.
  • Day 2: Content Organization & Sitemaps (2 hours)
    • Organize content through card sorting, build site maps.
  • Day 3: User Flows (2 hours)
    • Map out user flows to ensure efficient task completion within your design.

Month 2: User Testing & UI Tools (24 hours)

  • Day 1: A/B Testing (2 hours)
    • Learn how to run A/B tests to compare different versions of a design.
  • Day 2: Remote Usability Testing (2 hours)
    • Conduct usability testing remotely to gather valuable user feedback.
  • Day 3: Guerrilla Testing (2 hours)
    • Understand quick and informal testing methods with real users.
  • Day 1: Figma (2 hours)
    • Explore the basics of Figma for interface design and collaboration.
  • Day 2: Adobe XD (2 hours)
    • Learn the vector-based interface and prototype tools in Adobe XD.
  • Day 3: Adobe Photoshop (2 hours)
    • Use Photoshop to edit raster images for UI designs.
  • Day 1: Adobe Illustrator (2 hours)
    • Create vector graphics and icons for UI using Illustrator.
  • Day 2: Miro Board & FigJam (2 hours)
    • Utilize Miro and FigJam for collaborative whiteboarding and ideation.
  • Day 3: Design Systems in Figma (2 hours)
    • Explore creating reusable components and design systems in Figma.
  • Day 1: Heuristic Principles & UX Laws (2 hours)
    • Learn key heuristic principles and UX laws to enhance design decisions.
  • Day 2: UX Case Studies & Cognitive Bias (2 hours)
    • Analyze case studies and understand cognitive biases in design.
  • Day 3: Minimalism & Maximalism in UI (2 hours)
    • Explore how minimalist and maximalist design approaches can impact UX.

Month 3: UI Principles & Interaction Design (24 hours)

  • Day 1: Typography in UI (2 hours)
    • Learn how to use typography to create visual hierarchy and readability.
  • Day 2: Color Theory in UI (2 hours)
    • Study the psychology of colors and how to choose effective color palettes.
  • Day 3: Navigation in UI (2 hours)
    • Create intuitive and efficient navigation systems for better user flow.
  • Day 1: Gestalt Principles (2 hours)
    • Learn Gestalt principles to create visually cohesive designs.
  • Day 2: Responsive Design Systems (2 hours)
    • Apply breakpoints and media queries to create responsive layouts.
  • Day 3: Grid Layouts in UI Design (2 hours)
    • Design structured and balanced UI screens using grid systems.
  • Day 1: Prototyping in Figma (2 hours)
    • Build interactive prototypes in Figma for user testing and validation.
  • Day 2: Wireframing in Figma (2 hours)
    • Create low-fidelity wireframes to map out your designs.
  • Day 3: Designing UI Screens (2 hours)
    • Design individual UI screens and ensure consistency across devices.
  • Day 1: Microinteractions in UI (2 hours)
    • Learn to design subtle, engaging interactions to improve user experience.
  • Day 2: Animation in UI (2 hours)
    • Explore animation techniques to guide users through the interface.
  • Day 3: Usability & Accessibility in UI (2 hours)
    • Adhere to WCAG guidelines to ensure your designs are usable and accessible.

Month 4: Job Market Preparation (24 hours)

  • Day 1: Creating UX Case Studies for Portfolio (2 hours)
    • Learn how to showcase UX research, ideation, and design process in your portfolio.
  • Day 2: Crafting UI Screens for Portfolio (2 hours)
    • Design polished UI screens to display in your portfolio.
  • Day 3: Creating Interactive Prototypes for Portfolio (2 hours)
    • Create clickable prototypes to demonstrate the flow of your designs.
  • Day 1: The UX/UI Interview Process (2 hours)
    • Understand the stages of the interview process, from design challenges to panel interviews.
  • Day 2: Mock Interviews & Whiteboarding Challenges (2 hours)
    • Practice mock interviews and whiteboarding challenges to solve design problems.
  • Day 3: Industry Standards in Developer Handoff (2 hours)
    • Learn how to prepare and document design handoffs to developers.
  • Day 1: Documentation for UX/UI Projects (2 hours)
    • Learn to document your design decisions and user flows for teams and stakeholders.
  • Day 2: Tips for Developer Collaboration (2 hours)
    • Gain best practices for working closely with developers during the handoff phase.
  • Day 3: Handling Incomplete Design Tasks (2 hours)
    • Strategies for managing and delivering incomplete tasks in real-world projects.
  • Day 1: Portfolio Review (2 hours)
    • Get feedback on your design portfolio from mentors or peers.
  • Day 2: Mock Interview Practice (2 hours)
    • Participate in final mock interviews to prepare for job applications.
  • Day 3: Final Project Submission & Feedback (2 hours)
    • Present a final design project to demonstrate everything you’ve learned.
